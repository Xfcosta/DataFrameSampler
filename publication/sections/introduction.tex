\section{Introduction}

Tabular datasets remain central in business analytics, health informatics,
scientific computing, and machine learning. Developers and analysts often need
example tables that resemble existing data for tests, dashboards, training
material, and demonstrations. Direct reuse of source data may be inappropriate
because of governance, licensing, privacy, or operational constraints.

DataFrameSampler targets this pragmatic setting. It is not a formal privacy
mechanism and it is not a claim of state-of-the-art synthetic-data fidelity.
Instead, it provides a small, inspectable generator whose assumptions are
visible in ordinary Python code.

Version 2.0 uses a supervised latent representation throughout. Non-binary
numeric columns are standardized. Every categorical
column, including booleans and numeric two-valued columns such as $0/1$, is
given its own NCA block learned from the remaining columns. Generated latent
points are decoded back to the original schema with numeric inverse scaling and
probabilistic categorical classifiers.

The contributions are:
\begin{itemize}
  \item a direct \texttt{DataFrameSampler} estimator with \texttt{fit},
  \texttt{transform}, \texttt{inverse\_transform}, and \texttt{generate};
  \item an iterative per-categorical-column NCA latent construction;
  \item a local mutual-neighbour transport generator in the learned space;
  \item claim-specific diagnostics for distributional similarity, downstream
  utility, neighbour-distance behaviour, NCA-block mechanism evidence, decoder
  calibration, and extrapolative manifold consistency.
\end{itemize}

\section{Limitations}

DataFrameSampler has no formal privacy guarantee. It should not be presented as
differential privacy, formal de-identification, regulatory compliance, or
protection against linkage attacks, because none of these properties follows
from decoding new rows through a learned latent representation. In practice,
users who need a release mechanism must add an explicit privacy model and
disclosure-risk evaluation outside the sampler.

The method also has no global optimization objective. Per-column NCA blocks can
conflict, and the concatenated latent space is only an empirical construction
whose usefulness depends on the dataset and the configured widths. High-cardinality
categorical columns are used after a warning, so identifiers or near-identifiers
should be preprocessed deliberately rather than treated as ordinary categories.

Generation remains a local linear transport heuristic. It can produce
implausible rows when the learned latent space is poorly conditioned, when
categorical decoders are uncertain, or when real-world constraints span
multiple columns. Fitted min/max and numeric standard-deviation checks reduce
some obvious numeric failures by rejection and retry, however they are not a
general constraint solver. NCA-block ablations, decoder probability diagnostics,
and manifold stress therefore provide evidence about observed behaviour, not
proofs of causal mechanism quality.

Synthetic tabular data is often needed in settings where realistic examples are
useful for tests, demonstrations, notebooks, and exploratory modelling, but
direct reuse of source records is constrained by governance, licensing, privacy,
or operational policy. We present DataFrameSampler 2.0, a lightweight Python
library for mixed-type pandas dataframes that addresses this practical use case
without claiming formal privacy protection or general-purpose generative
optimality. The method constructs a fully numerical latent representation by
fitting one supervised Neighborhood Components Analysis block for each
categorical column, treating binary variables as categorical even when they are
stored numerically. Generation then applies a local mutual-neighbour translation
in the learned latent space and decodes the result with inverse numeric scaling
and probabilistic random-forest categorical decoders. More precisely,
DataFrameSampler is intended to make a small, inspectable construction feasible
for low-stakes example-data workflows; therefore we evaluate it with
claim-specific diagnostics rather than with a single aggregate benchmark. These
diagnostics include NCA-block ablations, decoder calibration checks, and
frozen-Isomap insertion stress, and they support only bounded empirical claims:
the method has no global objective, no formal manifold-preservation guarantee,
and no privacy guarantee.

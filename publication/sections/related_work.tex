\section{Related Work}

Synthetic tabular data generation has been studied using a range of approaches,
including probabilistic graphical models, copulas, nearest-neighbour resampling,
and deep generative models. Deep architectures such as CTGAN and related
methods have shown that adversarial and neural density models can capture
complex dependencies in mixed-type tabular data \cite{xu2019ctgan}. Broader
software systems for synthetic data generation provide practical interfaces for
training, sampling, and evaluating such models \cite{patki2016sdv}.

The planned empirical comparison separates simple transparent baselines from
model-based competitors. Simple baselines include row bootstrap sampling,
independent column-wise sampling, copula-style numeric modelling with empirical
categorical sampling, and supervised oversampling methods such as SMOTE or
SMOTENC when a target label is present. Model-based competitors include common
Synthetic Data Vault families such as GaussianCopula, CTGAN, TVAE, and
CopulaGAN. This separation is important for the paper's claim: the relevant
question is not only whether DataFrameSampler wins a single fidelity metric, but
whether it offers a useful trade-off among ease of use, inspectability,
distributional similarity, and configuration burden.

DataFrameSampler occupies a different point in the design space. Rather than
training a large generative model, it uses discretization, local neighbour
relationships, and bin-level decoding. This makes the method comparatively
simple and transparent, while limiting the strength of claims that can be made
about distributional fidelity or privacy.

Privacy-preserving synthetic data and statistical disclosure control address a
stronger problem than this paper claims to solve. Differential privacy,
formal de-identification, and regulated clinical data release require explicit
privacy definitions, threat models, and disclosure-risk evaluation. The
sensitive-value replacement layer in DataFrameSampler is therefore positioned
only as a safeguard against exact re-use of selected values, not as a formal
privacy-preserving generator.

The method is also related to classical interpolation and oversampling
approaches such as SMOTE \cite{chawla2002smote}, which create new examples by
operating in a feature space defined by local neighbours. DataFrameSampler uses
a related local-neighbour intuition, but applies it to a discretized latent
representation of entire dataframe rows and decodes generated bins back to
observed mixed-type values.

Finally, the package relies on standard dimensionality reduction and
nearest-neighbour search components from the Python scientific computing
ecosystem, including scikit-learn \cite{pedregosa2011scikit}. Optional
approximate-nearest-neighbour backends such as NN-Descent, HNSW, and Annoy can
be used for larger datasets \cite{dong2011efficient,malkov2018hnsw,bernhardsson2015annoy}.

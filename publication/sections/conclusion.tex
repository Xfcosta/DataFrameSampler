\section{Conclusion}

DataFrameSampler 2.0 provides a lightweight mixed-type dataframe sampler based
on iterative supervised NCA latent blocks and local mutual-neighbour transport.
The resulting estimator has a direct sklearn-style API and a file-oriented CLI,
but its modelling claim is intentionally narrow: it is a simple and inspectable
tool for example-data workflows, not a formally private or globally optimized
synthetic-data mechanism.

The mechanism and calibration diagnostics make this conclusion more precise.
Categorical NCA blocks are supported only where they improve held-out
categorical prediction against simple baselines, decoder probabilities should be
interpreted through their measured calibration quality, and manifold evidence
should be read as an insertion-stress diagnostic rather than as a preservation
guarantee. Future work should therefore focus on stability of the iterative
latent construction, constraint-aware decoding, stronger high-cardinality
handling, and alternatives to independent per-column decoders.

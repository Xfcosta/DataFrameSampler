\section{Conclusion}

This paper stub introduced DataFrameSampler, a lightweight package for
generating mixed-type tabular samples through vectorization, discretization,
local neighbour sampling, and bin-level decoding. The implementation provides a
practical Python API and CLI, configurable embedding and nearest-neighbour
backends, CSV/Parquet I/O, optional LLM-assisted configuration, and
sensitive-value replacement before sampling.

The central argument is that simple and inspectable generation has value in its
own right. For business and medical software workflows, a human-understandable
mechanism can be preferable when the goal is to create examples for testing,
demonstration, training, and exploratory modelling under explicit governance
constraints.

The next step is to complete the empirical evaluation and characterize where
the method is useful, where it fails, and how its configuration choices affect
nearest-neighbour closeness, real-versus-synthetic distinguishability, utility
lift, distribution similarity, and ease of review.

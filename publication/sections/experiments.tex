\section{Experiments}

The empirical difficulty is that DataFrameSampler makes several different
claims, none of which is adequately tested by a single benchmark score. We
therefore evaluate it as a practical example-data generator rather than as a
universal synthetic-data winner. More precisely, the experiments ask whether
1) generated rows avoid being pathologically close to real rows, 2) generated
rows preserve useful distributional and task information, 3) the supervised NCA
blocks provide measurable categorical structure, 4) decoder probabilities are
well behaved enough to be interpreted diagnostically, and 5) extrapolative
points remain compatible with a frozen manifold geometry.

Current comparisons include DataFrameSampler plus simple baselines: row bootstrap, independent columns, Gaussian-copula
empirical sampling, and target-stratified column sampling where a target exists.
These baselines are intentionally not exhaustive; instead, they define concrete
failure modes that are relevant to the paper's restricted claims, such as direct
row reuse, broken dependencies, or low-cost empirical marginal sampling.

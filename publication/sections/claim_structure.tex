\section{Evaluation Framework}
\label{sec:evaluation-framework}

The evaluation is organized around claims rather than around a single
leaderboard. This choice is central to the paper because the intended
contribution is bounded: DataFrameSampler is proposed as an inspectable
example-data generator, not as a general synthetic-data optimum, a privacy
mechanism, or a guaranteed manifold sampler. Consequently, each empirical
quantity is assigned a role. Primary evidence tests the practical-use claim
directly; supporting evidence probes the mechanism; and boundary evidence
identifies conditions under which the claim should be weakened.

This framework also determines the allowable conclusion. If primary evidence
is missing, the practical claim must be weakened. If supporting evidence is
missing, the mechanism remains plausible rather than supported. If boundary
evidence is missing, the scope of use should be narrowed. The experiments
therefore ask whether the sampler behaves usefully under several diagnostic
views at once, not whether one favourable metric can be selected after the
fact.
